Legacy Wellsite Scoping Project

Wood PLC, Brisbane

Role: Integrity Engineer Intern

Internship Duration: September 2024 - January 2026

Key skills: Mechanical Drawings • NDT Reports • Nexus IC • Draw.io • Adobe Acrobat Pro

As part of my internship at Wood PLC, I contributed to a project focused on developing new inspection scopes for legacy wellsites that had previously been inspected without a scope. This project was a result of historically inconsistent inspections at these vessels, which reduces the amount of useful data that can be extracted and extrapolated from. My contribution to this project involved creating over 100 inspection scopes for a range of equipment types, including pressure vessels, filters, and heat exchangers, while ensuring that inspection locations were sufficient and consistent, with further examples attached below.


This task required significant attention to detail as oftentimes the mechanical drawings and NDT reports were incomplete or inconsistent, requiring me to make informed assumptions and judgement to fill in the gaps. I also had to ensure that the scopes I created were compatible with the Nexus IC mobile inspection system, which involved understanding the components of the system (e.g. shell, head, nozzles, etc.) and what information they required.

Throughout this project, I was able to develop key skills in:

  • Interpreting mechanical drawings of a range of equipment types, including pressure vessels, filters, and heat exchangers
  • Identifying important monitoring locations from interpretation of historical NDT reports and damage mechanisms
  • Developing organised inspection scopes in Draw.io by creating simple schematics
  • Connecting inspection scopes to asset integrity management databases
